Personality Traits and Film Preferences Analysis

Introduction

This study examines if personality traits play a role in influencing film preferences. Using the Personality 2018 dataset of GroupLens, the following research questions are addressed:

  • Do certain personality traits (e.g., extraversion, openness) correlate with film choices?
  • Do specific genres (comedy, horror, sci-fi, etc.) appeal to certain personality traits?
  • Can this data be used for targeted marketing campaigns?

Correlation Heatmap (Personality Traits vs. Ratings)

This informs us whether personality traits at all influence ratings for movies.

Key Findings:

  • No strong correlations between movie ratings and personality traits.
  • Agreeableness is highest (0.06), very weak.
  • Conclusion: According to the heatmap, personality traits do not have a strong influence on how highly people rate movies.

Average Personality Traits per Genre

This plot examines whether there are different kinds of users per genre.

Key Findings:

  • Openness is high for all genres, with minimal variation.
  • Extraversion is always lower, in agreement with previous results that users are more introverted.
  • Conclusion: There is no significant evidence to suggest that personality traits influence genre preferences.

Ratings per Genre by High/Low Openness

This plot explores if users with high levels of openness rank genres differently than low-openness users.

Key Findings:

  • individuals exhibiting high openness scores score Mystery and Sci-Fi slightly higher.
  • No significant effect of openness on other genres.
  • Conclusion: Openness has a very minimal effect on genre ratings, which is not sufficient for targeted marketing.

Ratings per Genre by High/Low Extraversion

This plot explores to see if extroverted users score genres differently compared to introverted users.

Key Findings:

  • No differences of importance in ratings between extroverted users and introverted.
  • Conclusion: Extraversion has no significant impact on movie ratings.

Conclusion

Do personality traits influence film choice?

  • No significant correlation was identified between personality traits and ratings.
  • There are no clear personality types that are drawn to different genres.
  • Horror and children's film ratings have the most extreme differences between viewers, but this disparity doesn't seem to be along personality types.

Marketing Implications:

  • Rather than pitching by personality type, focus on genre engagement trends instead.
  • Mystery and Film-Noir genres can be sold as highly rated choices.
  • Horror and Children's films require more targeted marketing, as they evoke the most polarizing reactions.

In conclusion, this study does not validate the premise that personality traits and film selection are correlated. Instead, marketing must be done on the basis of genre-based participation..
