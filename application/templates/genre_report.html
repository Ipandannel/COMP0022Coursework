Genre Popularity & Polarization Report

Introduction

This paper examines the popularity and polarization of various film genres according to audience ratings. The goal is to determine which genres consistently receive high ratings and which evoke the most polarized ratings. This data can help festival programmers to program movie screenings more efficiently.

Methodology

The data set includes ratings from various users of films. Two key factors are studied in this research:

  • Popularity: Measured by the average rating per genre and total number of ratings.
  • Polarization: Measured by the standard deviation of ratings, which shows how much opinions differ.

Genre Popularity Analysis

Average Ratings by Genre

The following chart displays the average ratings per genre, indicating the highest-rated categories.

  • Top-rated genres: The highest rated genres are Documentary, War, and Film-Noir.
  • Lowest-rated genres: Comedy and Horror genres have lower average ratings, despite being highly popular.

Number of Ratings by Genre

This graph shows the most popular genres with audiences, by the number of ratings they have received.

  • Most-rated genres: Drama, Comedy, and Action are the most highly rated, suggesting maximum engagement.
  • Least-rated genres: Most specialized genres like Film-Noir and Western receive lower ratings.

Genre Polarization Analysis

Most Polarizing Genres

The following graph shows the genre-by-genre standard deviation of ratings, which highlights genres with the most divergent opinions.

  • Most polarizing genres: Horror, Sci-Fi, and Comedy are the most varying-rated genres, i.e., the genres most frequently loved or hated.
  • Least polarizing genres: Documentary and Film-Noir have the lowest variability, which means stable audience opinions.

Rating Distribution by Genre (Box Plot)

A box plot gives further information on the distribution of ratings within genres.

  • Wider boxes and longer whiskers signify more disagreement within a genre.
  • Outliers have extreme opinions (very low or very high scores).

Conclusion

It emphasizes key trends in genre popularity and audience attitude:

  • Documentary, War, and Film-Noir are the most highly rated genres, indicating that these genres are specialized but highly popular.
  • Comedy and Drama are most viewed, yet Comedy is also very polarizing.
  • Horror and Sci-Fi are most polarizing, i.e., they produce strong sentiments (love/hate).

Such observations can assist film festival programmers in programming decisions that balance highly regarded films with controversial, debate-provoking films.
